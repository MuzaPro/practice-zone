3 Electrode Configuration

This setup has become the gold standard for accurate electrochemical measurements. This arrangement allows precise control of the potential at the working electrode while accurately measuring the resulting current. The separation of potential control and current measurement functions is what makes this system so powerful for investigating catalytic processes, reaction mechanisms, and material properties.

Electrode Introduction

The working electrode (WE)

is where the electrochemical event of interest occurs.

The reference electrode (RE)

has a well-defined and stable equilibrium potential. It functions as a reference point against which the potential of the working electrode is measured, without passing any significant current itself.

The counter electrode (CE)

completes the electrical circuit, allowing current to flow. When reduction occurs at the working electrode, oxidation happens at the counter electrode (and vice versa).
